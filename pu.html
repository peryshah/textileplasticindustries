What is Polyurethane (PU)?

Polyurethane (PU) is a highly versatile polymer known for its unique combination of properties, including:

  • High Elasticity: Ideal for applications requiring flexibility and resilience.
  • Excellent Abrasion Resistance: Perfect for components subjected to wear and tear.
  • Chemical Resistance: Resistant to oils, solvents, and many chemicals, making it suitable for harsh environments.
  • Load-Bearing Capacity: Provides excellent shock absorption and load distribution.

Polyurethane is widely used in industries such as automotive, construction, manufacturing, and more, making it a reliable choice for demanding applications.

Applications of Polyurethane (PU)

  • Automotive: Bushings, seals, and suspension components for durability and shock absorption.
  • Construction: Seals, gaskets, and insulation materials for weather resistance and durability.
  • Manufacturing: Rollers, wheels, and conveyor belts for wear-resistant applications.
  • Consumer Goods: Footwear, sports equipment, and flexible molds for comfort and performance.
