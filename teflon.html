What is Teflon?

Teflon, also known as Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), is a high-performance fluoropolymer known for its unique properties, including:

  • Non-Stick Surface: Ideal for applications requiring low friction and easy release.
  • Chemical Resistance: Resistant to almost all chemicals, making it suitable for harsh environments.
  • High-Temperature Tolerance: Can withstand extreme temperatures, both high and low.
  • Electrical Insulation: Excellent dielectric properties for electrical and electronic applications.

Teflon is widely used in industries such as food processing, chemical, automotive, aerospace, and more, making it a go-to material for demanding applications.

Applications of Teflon

  • Food Processing: Non-stick coatings for baking trays, conveyor belts, and molds.
  • Chemical Industry: Linings for tanks, pipes, and valves due to its chemical resistance.
  • Automotive: Seals, gaskets, and bearings for high-temperature and low-friction applications.
  • Aerospace: Insulation for wires and cables, as well as components requiring lightweight and durable materials.
